How it works

The service checks the same pages your team would normally monitor by hand, compares each version with the last one and sends a short alert when something changed.

1

Track

Follow the GOV.UK and SIA pages that matter to renewals, training and licence checks.

2

Compare

Compare each fetch with the last stored version and ignore pages that did not materially change.

3

Summarise

Pull out the lines that matter and label the update so someone in ops can understand it quickly.

4

Alert

Send the result into email, Telegram or your internal feed and keep a record of what changed.

Common questions

          
The main objections are trust, scope and whether this replaces legal advice. Handle those directly and keep the answers plain.

        

      

      

        
Is this already live?
Yes. The monitor is already deployed as a scheduled watchdog and is running autonomously on a recurring interval.

        
How is this different from checking GOV.UK yourself?
It stores baseline snapshots, detects material changes, extracts the relevant lines and only alerts when something changed. That removes routine checking work entirely.

        
Is this legal advice?
No. It is a monitoring and evidence-linking product. It tells operators what changed, where it changed and why it likely matters operationally.
