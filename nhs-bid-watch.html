What the service does

NHS Bid Watch monitors the procurement pages your team would otherwise check manually, then flags the items that matter: new tenders, award notices, framework openings and buyer movement.

01

Early bid signals

Catch new opportunities and route them to the team before deadlines get tight or notices disappear into inbox clutter.

02

Source-linked evidence

Each alert points back to the source notice, buyer and category so someone in commercial or bids can verify it quickly.

03

Buyer-focused triage

Segment signals by supplier type, geography or category so your team sees fewer irrelevant notices and more likely fits.

How the service works

The service checks NHS procurement surfaces, compares new notices with the last saved state, extracts the commercial details and sends the result into the channel your bid team already uses.

1

Track

Watch Contracts Finder, Find a Tender, NHS SBS, CCS and selected Trust or ICB procurement pages.

2

Filter

Remove duplicates and low-fit notices, then rank the remaining signals by category, buyer and likely relevance.

3

Summarise

Pull out the deadline, buyer, category and notice type so the team can decide quickly whether to act.

4

Alert

Send the opportunity into email, Telegram or an internal bid feed with a short plain-English summary.

Common questions

These are the first questions most suppliers ask before trialling the service.

Who is this for?

Suppliers selling into the NHS: medtech, cyber, IT, staffing and service providers that need earlier visibility into bid activity.

Does this replace a bid manager?

No. It helps the team find and triage opportunities faster. The commercial decision and response still sit with the supplier.

Why not use generic tender portals?

Because generic portals are noisy. NHS Bid Watch is meant to surface the notices, buyers and framework movement that fit your category more closely.
